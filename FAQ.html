Frequently Asked Questions

What Is CoVID-19?

CoVID-19 is a pandemic caused by the coronavirus. Coronaviruses are a family of virus known for causing respiratory diseases that can be as mild as a common cold, or as severe as MERS or SARS. The first case of CoVID-19 was found in Wuhan China in December 2019. Today, over 150 000 indivuidauls have been infected, and that number is continuing to rise as the disease spreads across the globe. Currently, there is no treatment to cure the coronavirus.

Who Is A Pandemic?

A pandemic is an illness or disease that spreads to multiple countries around the globe. It is different from an epidemic as it is not about the number of cases, but the geographical location of cases.

I heard Africans are safe. Is this true?

No, this is not true. Africans are just as likely to be infected as indivudals from different countries. Although countries in Africa have the least amount of cases, that is quickly changing as CoVID-19 spreads across the globe.

Who Is Most At Risk?

The following increase an individuals chance of being infected with CoVID-19

  • Being over 65 years old
  • Having heart or lung disease
  • diabetes
  • smoking
  • malnourishment

These indivuals should take extra precaution to protect themselves.

How Can I Protect Myself?

to take precaution

  • Frequently wash your hands thoroughly for at least 15 seconds
  • Do not touch your face
  • Cover your mouth when you cough or sneeze with you elbow
  • stay indoors if you show any symptoms or might be infected with CoVID-19. Contact authorities

If you show symptoms of CoVID-19 please contact emergency and health care aid.

How Is CoVID-19 Spread?

It is spread through droplets released when an infected person coughs or sneezes. These droplets are then inhaled and infect, or they land on surfaces that infect after others touch the surface and then their face

How many people have been infected so far?

As of March 15th over 150 000 individuals have been infected worldwide, and around 6000 have died. This number is rising by thousands each day.

Should I be Worried?

No, please do not be worried, but simply take precaution to protect yourself as well as those around you.

How Can I help?

You can help by following the advice of health care and emergency workers, as well as any advice on this site. Continue to follow guidlines on how to protect yourself and your community. If you are infected or have been in contact with someone who is please self isolate for 14 days.

All donations will be greatly appreciated and help the efforts made towards responding to CoVID-19 in Somalia.
